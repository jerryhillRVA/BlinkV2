# Discovery Question Bank

Use these questions as guidance. You do not need to ask every question — adapt based on what the user shares. Ask follow-ups when answers are vague.

## 01 — Business Overview

**Core Questions:**
- In one to two sentences, what does your business do — and who does it serve?
- What are your top 1-3 business goals for the next 12 months?
- What is the single most important commercial outcome you want content to support? (e.g., product sign-ups, community membership, email list, brand awareness)
- Where is the business right now — and where do you want it to be in 12 months? Include any numbers you're comfortable sharing (followers, revenue, traffic, community size).

**Follow-up Prompts:**
- What's the one thing about your business that most people misunderstand or underestimate?
- Is there a specific product, service, or initiative content needs to support right now?

## 02 — Brand & Voice

**Core Questions:**
- Describe your brand's personality in 3-5 words or phrases.
- What 3 words or phrases should your content NEVER sound like?
- What is your brand's core belief — the one thing you stand for?
- Is there a brand, creator, or publication whose tone you admire? What do they do that works?

**Follow-up Prompts:**
- What topic or format do you feel most confident creating content around?
- What topic or format do you most want to avoid?
- Is there anything about how your brand has shown up online that you'd like to leave behind?

## 03 — Audience

**Core Questions:**
- Describe your primary audience. Who are they, and why do they care about what you do?
- What is the #1 problem, frustration, or question your audience has that your content should help with?
- What does your audience already believe about your topic that may or may not be true?
- If your audience could only take one action after engaging with your content, what would you want it to be?

**Follow-up Prompts:**
- Do you have a meaningful secondary audience? If so, how do they differ?
- What age range, life stage, or profession describes your primary audience?
- Where does your audience currently go for information on this topic?

## 04 — Competitors

**Core Questions:**
- Who are the 3-5 creators, brands, or companies your audience also follows or reads?
- What do your top 1-2 competitors do really well in their content?
- Where do you see a gap — something your competitors are NOT doing that your audience clearly wants?

**Follow-up Prompts:**
- Which competitor do you respect most — and what specifically do they do well?
- Which competitor do you most want to be clearly differentiated from — and why?

## 05 — Content Strategy

**Core Questions:**
- What content have you already created or published? List any platforms and approximate volume.
- Has any piece of content performed noticeably well? What was it and why do you think it resonated?
- What kind of content do you most want to create? What formats feel natural or exciting?
- What topic areas do you feel most confident and energized talking about?

**Follow-up Prompts:**
- Is there any topic, format, or approach you're NOT willing to create content around?
- Do you have existing content assets (blog posts, videos, courses) that could be repurposed?

## 06 — Channels & Capacity

**Core Questions:**
- Which platforms are you currently active on and which do you want to prioritize?
- What is your realistic content production capacity per week?
- Who will be involved in content creation and what are their roles?

**Follow-up Prompts:**
- Which platform is your single best-performing right now, and what makes it work?
- Which platform do you most want to grow and why?
- Have you tried any platforms or formats that didn't work out? What happened?

## 07 — Expectations & Goals

**Core Questions:**
- What does success look like to you at the 90-day mark? Describe both a metric and a feeling.
- What's the single most important thing you want to walk away from this session with?

**Follow-up Prompts:**
- Is there anything about your business, brand, or content situation that hasn't been covered?
- Are there any constraints (budget, time, team size) that we should factor into the strategy?
